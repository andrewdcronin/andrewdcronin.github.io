I am a evolutionary and behavioral ecologist fascinated by how animals cope with environmental variation. My research integrates animal behavior, sexual selection, phenotypic plasticity, and evolutionary ecology to understand how organisms respond to variation and change in the environment. To address these questions, I integrate experimental and field-based approaches using amphibians as my main model system. I am currently an NSF Postdoctoral Fellow working with University of North Carolina, Chapel Hill in the lab of Dr. David Pfennig and Dr. Karin Pfennig, and the Smithsonian Tropical Research Institute.
